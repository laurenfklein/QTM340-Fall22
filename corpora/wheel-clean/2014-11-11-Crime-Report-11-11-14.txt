On Nov. 4 at 6:40 a.m., Emory Police Department (EPD) responded to a call regarding an individual sleeping in the women’s restroom on the third floor of the Dobbs University Center. The complainant said that a female was sleeping on a sofa in the bathroom. The individual had taken off her jeans and put them on a hanger. Officers arrived on the scene and checked the bathroom, but they could not locate anyone.On Nov. 4 at 2:15 p.m., EPD responded to a call regarding a stolen laptop on the third floor of AtwoodHall. The complainant, who was an administrative assistant, said that when she returned from a meeting,her laptop was missing. There was a suspicious subject who was seen in the area, but EPD was unable to locate the individual. Information regarding the case has been turned over to a detective.On Nov. 5 at 2:34 p.m., EPD responded to a call regarding a theft at the Callaway building. A staff member at the location said some one stole his laptop valued at $2,300. He also had his iPhone stolen,valued at $600. The case has been turned over to an investigator.On Nov. 5 at 8:34 p.m., EPD responded to a call regarding suspicious activity on the fifth floor of the Robert W. Woodruff Library. The security officer on the location said an individual turned over a bag of what appeared to be marijuana to him. The individual said the marijuana was left unattended on a table in the library. Officers confiscated the marijuana when they arrived on the location.On Nov. 6 at 5:45 p.m., EPD responded to a fire alarm on he second floor of Clairmont Tower. Someoneburned food at the location. Officers examined the area and did not find anything of concern. The alarm was reset.— Compiled by Crime Beat Writer Brandon Fuhr
